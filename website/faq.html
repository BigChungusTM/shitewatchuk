About This Project

ShitWatch UK monitors real-time storm overflow events across England using data from water company APIs. We track ALL completed sewage discharges regardless of duration and publish detailed information every 90 minutes, providing complete transparency.

How It Works

  1. Continuous Monitoring: We check water company APIs every 1 minute for ongoing discharge events
  2. Duration Tracking: Events are tracked from start to finish, with duration calculated in real-time
  3. Complete Coverage: ALL completed events are catalogued - no minimum duration required
  4. 90-Minute Cycles: Every 90 minutes, we publish all completed events and post to Twitter
  5. AI Summaries: Each cycle includes an AI-generated summary explaining what the data means
  6. Worst Offenders Highlighted: Twitter posts highlight the longest duration events to drive awareness

Data Sources

  • Water Company APIs: Real-time data from all 6 major water companies
    • Anglian Water
    • Thames Water
    • United Utilities
    • Yorkshire Water
    • Southern Water
    • Severn Trent Water
  • Historical Flow Rates: Permit database analysis for volume estimates
  • Site Locations: GPS coordinates from EA discharge consents

What Data Do We Show?

Each 90-minute cycle shows detailed information about sewage discharge events:

  • Water company responsible
  • Discharge site ID and location (GPS coordinates)
  • Receiving watercourse (river, stream, etc.)
  • Start time, end time, and total duration
  • Google Maps links to see exact locations
  • AI-generated summary of impacts and context
